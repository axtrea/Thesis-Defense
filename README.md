# Development of Noise-Robust Topological Gene Expression Deconvolution Approach
In silico cell-type deconvolution from bulk transcriptomics data is a powerful approach for inferring the cellular composition of complex tissues. Among existing methods, DualSimplex provides a unified analytical framework for deconvolution 
that leverages the topological properties of gene expression space to achieve accurate cell-type proportion estimates. However, the performance of DualSimplex is highly sensitive to the presence of extreme noise and outliers.  
In this work, we propose a noise-robust topological gene expression approach that integrates an iterative density-based filtering strategy into the deconvolution pipeline. The method is designed to identify and remove low-density observations that are likely to correspond to outliers, while adaptively recalculating neighborhood 
radius and density estimates at each iteration.
